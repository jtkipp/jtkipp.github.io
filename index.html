Introduction

Software development is often treated as a ablity with a large amount of je ne said quoi. The most famous facetious explanation of the quality developer attitude is, "laziness, hubris." In reality the opposite is true. but the skills and attitudes of successful software development are both learnable and coachable.

The first manager a software developer has when he or she begins full time development will have a huge impact on the software that developer will produce for the rest of their career. Nobody begins developing software with a natural understanding of what makes clear, easily maintainable source code. Rather the software they read will create an imprint of how software should look that will take a great deal of conscious effort to change.
